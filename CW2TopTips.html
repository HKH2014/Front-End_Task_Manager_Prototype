Top Tips for Completing Tasks



A great way to get started is a skill called ‘chunking,’ which means breaking things down into small actionable steps.

Narrow your list down to just three items as sometimes huge to-do lists can be overwhelming — not to mention impossible to get done in one day.

Have an alarm to stop yourself from scrolling Instagram or TikTok, which can eat into the time you would have to complete tasks and do chores.


Redefine success of a task to make it more manageable or easier. An example is to tell yourself that washing half of the dishes today will count as a win.

Breaking the task down into small do-able parts that get paced out can help break down that wall of total resistance.

Transition to the task by doing another activity first - using another activity to help you transition to the task you want to do makes the task easier to get into.
